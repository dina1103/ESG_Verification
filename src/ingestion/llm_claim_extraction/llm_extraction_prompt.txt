A claim is any statement related to Environmental, Social, or Governance (ESG) topics that fits one of these types:
- target: a future goal with a specific year or timeframe (e.g., "we will reach net zero by 2050")
- commitment: a stated intention or principle without a specific deadline (e.g., "we are committed to diversity")
- achievement: a past or present accomplishment, possibly with quantified results (e.g., "we reduced emissions by 30% in 2023")
- narrative: a description of a SPECIFIC, NAMED ESG program, policy, or initiative the company runs (e.g., a named training program, a defined diversity initiative, a specific code of conduct). Generic descriptions of organizational structures, business processes, or how the company operates day-to-day are NOT claims and should be skipped.

2. claim_type — one of: "target", "commitment", "achievement", "narrative"
3. metric — the thing being measured (e.g., "GHG emissions", "water use", "anti-corruption training", "renewable electricity share"). Use "N/A" if no specific metric.
4. quantified_value — a number if the claim contains a numeric value, otherwise null
5. unit — the unit of the value (e.g., "%", "tCO2e", "m3", "employees"), or null if no value or unit not stated
6. baseline_year — integer if the claim references a baseline year, otherwise null
7. baseline_value — number if a baseline value is stated, otherwise null
8. target_year — integer if the claim has a target year, otherwise null
9. scope — one of: "Scope 1", "Scope 2", "Scope 3", "Scope 1+2", "Scope 1+2+3", "N/A"

   Inference rules (used when scope is not stated):

   - Scope 2: indirect emissions from purchased energy (e.g., purchased electricity, steam, heating, cooling)
   - Scope 3: indirect emissions in the value chain (e.g., purchased goods and services, business travel, employee commuting, use of sold products, "supply chain emissions", "value chain emissions")
   - "Scope 1+2": claim covers both Scope 1 and Scope 2 (e.g., "Scope 1 and 2 emissions reduced by X%")
   - "Scope 1+2+3": claim covers all three (e.g., "total emissions across all scopes")

   Use "N/A" when:
   - The claim is not about GHG emissions (e.g., water use, anti-corruption training, recycled content)
   - The text mentions emissions but doesn't specify the scope and the scope cannot be inferred from context
